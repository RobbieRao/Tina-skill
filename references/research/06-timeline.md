## Research and Project Milestones

- 2009: Shoe-last design innovation for better shoe fitting.
  Source: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.compind.2009.05.015
  Impact: early example of using design + measurement to improve fit in wearable / body-adjacent products.

- 2010: A comparison between Chinese and Caucasian head shapes.
  Source: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.apergo.2010.02.002
  Impact: establishes population-specific head morphology as a practical design concern.

- 2011: The 3D Chinese head and face modeling.
  Source: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cad.2011.01.011
  Impact: turns anthropometric differences into 3D digital modeling infrastructure.

- 2011: Sizing and grading for wearable products.
  Source: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cad.2011.07.004
  Impact: expands fit logic into wearable product sizing systems.

- 2015 / 2016: RGC Early Career Scheme project on 3D human head and face model with soft tissue deformation.
  Source: PolyU profile project list.
  Impact: strengthens biomechanical / deformable modeling direction.

- 2016 / 2017: RGC GRF project on sizing and comfort for ear-related product design.
  Source: PolyU profile project list.
  Impact: extends from head shape to ear-related product comfort.

- 2017: SizeChina Faceform won Best Innovation Award from ACED Ergonomics Design Awards in Japan.
  Sources: PolyU profile and PolyU 2018 news.
  Impact: external validation of anthropometric design tools.

- 2018: Headsets: A design handbook for sound and shape, co-authored design handbook.
  Source: PolyU profile publication list.
  Impact: integrates product design, head/ear fit, sound, and shape.

- 2018: Three-dimensional human head modelling: a systematic review.
  Source: https://doi.org/10.1080/1463922X.2018.1432715
  Impact: synthesizes the technical domain around 3D head modeling.

- 2019: Older adults' use of mobile device: usability challenges while navigating various interfaces.
  Source: https://doi.org/10.1080/0144929X.2019.1622786
  Impact: broadens from physical fit to interface usability for older adults.

- 2020: AI enhanced 3D head scanning technology and related products development, funded by AiDLab / ITF.
  Source: PolyU profile project list.
  Impact: explicit turn toward AI-enabled design tooling.

- 2020: Trust in AI Agent systematic review of facial anthropomorphic trustworthiness for social robot design.
  Source: https://doi.org/10.3390/s20185087
  Impact: trust and social perception become measurable design variables.

- 2021 / 2022: Parametric biomechanical head models for product design and evaluation.
  Source: PolyU profile project list.
  Impact: models move from static surface geometry toward biomechanical evaluation.

- 2022: Customize My Helmet: algorithmic approach based on 3D head prediction.
  Source: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cad.2022.103271
  Impact: customization from partial scans / predicted head shape becomes concrete product workflow.

- 2022 / 2023: 3D dynamic human head, neck, and shoulder modelling for product design.
  Source: PolyU profile project list.
  Impact: moves from static fit toward motion / dynamics.

- 2023: Children's 3D head growth patterns and comfort models for eyeglasses.
  Sources: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.apergo.2022.103933 and https://doi.org/10.1016/j.apergo.2023.104054
  Impact: links growth, morphology, and comfort prediction.

- 2023: AdaptSpecs won IDEA Silver Award.
  Source: PolyU profile and RAE2026 PDF.
  Impact: inclusive eyewear system for users who cannot rely on regular nose/ear support points.

- 2023 / 2024: Social traits perception model on 3D human faces for face-related product design, funded by RGC GRF.
  Source: PolyU profile project list.
  Impact: connects physical faces with perceived social traits and product implications.

- 2024: AI-Driven Ergonomic Headwear Customization System won Gold Medal with Congratulations of the Jury at the 49th International Exhibition of Inventions Geneva.
  Sources: PolyU profile, AiDLab event page, PolyU news.
  Impact: international recognition of the automated headwear customization system.

- 2024 / 2025: 4D head movement prediction model for product design and evaluation.
  Source: PolyU profile project list.
  Impact: adds time and motion into head-related product design.

- 2025: AI powered ergonomic product design for neck and back health.
  Source: PolyU profile project list.
  Impact: extends AI + ergonomics beyond headwear toward neck/back health.

- 2025: Face2Wear, FunBreath, and other recent work show continued expansion into facewear personalization and health-oriented product systems.
