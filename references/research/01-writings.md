## Core Repeated Claims / Operating Beliefs

1. Fit and comfort should be treated as measurable relationships between human morphology, product parameters, and user perception.
2. Population-level human variation matters; Chinese, Caucasian, child, adult, sex, and age differences cannot be safely collapsed into a generic body model.
3. 3D digital human models are not decorative visualization; they are infrastructure for design decisions, CAD, sizing, and customization.
4. AI is useful when it lowers the cost of measurement, prediction, or parametric customization, not as a stand-alone design slogan.
5. Trust, usability, and comfort are perception problems that can be studied experimentally.

## Candidate Mental Models from Writings

- Human-model-before-product-model: build the user morphology model before optimizing the artifact.
- Quantified comfort: convert subjective comfort into measurable variables and predictive models.
- Population specificity: treat diversity as a design input, not a post-hoc adjustment.
- Parametric personalization: design systems should move from one-off prototypes to repeatable customization workflows.
- Trust as embodied perception: human response to AI / robot / interface cues can be measured and designed.
